Australia moves to ban social media access for users under 16

December 4, 2025

Australia's internet regulator has backed a new law to block social-media access for users under 16, citing concerns over teen mental health and rising online risks. The law targets platforms including Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat and YouTube, mandating that they deactivate or freeze accounts belonging to underage users starting December 10. Officials expect this move may set a precedent for global regulation of Big Tech and spark debate on free speech and youth protection. Critics have warned of enforcement challenges and possible backlash from families.

Rohingya refugee camps in Cox's Bazar face humanitarian crisis as aid funding plummets

December 2, 2025

Humanitarian aid to refugee camps in Cox's Bazar has decreased sharply, putting over a million Rohingya - most of them women and children - in a precarious situation. Many families lack basic necessities such as soap, food, clean water, and shelter. New arrivals receive minimal support, and infants are at high risk of malnutrition, while health services have become overwhelmed. Aid organisations are warning that without urgent intervention, suffering and preventable deaths may spike in coming weeks.

France and China sign sweeping trade and cooperation deals during Macron-Xi summit

December 4, 2025

At a high-profile summit in Beijing, French President and Chinese leader struck a series of agreements covering trade, green tech, aerospace, and energy cooperation. The pact includes €100 million in new aid toward global climate initiatives and a joint commitment to address global crises. While the diplomatic gesture aims to strengthen bilateral ties amid geopolitical uncertainty, the deals have sparked debates in the European Union over potential impacts on EU cohesion and alignment. Observers say the agreement underscores shifting global alliances as countries navigate a rapidly changing international landscape.
